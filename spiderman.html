Origin Story

Spiderman, also known as Peter Parker, gained his superpowers after being bitten by a radioactive spider.

Powers and Abilities

Spiderman possesses superhuman strength, agility, and the ability to cling to walls. He also has a "spider-sense" which warns him of danger.

Allies and Enemies

Spiderman works alongside other superheroes like Iron Man, Captain America, and Thor. His enemies include the Green Goblin, Venom, and Doctor Octopus.

Popular Culture

Spiderman has appeared in numerous comic books, movies, TV shows, and video games, making him one of the most iconic superheroes of all time.

Spider-Man is a Marvel Comics superhero character created by writer-editor Stan Lee and writer-artist Steve Ditko. He first appeared in the anthology comic book Amazing Fantasy #15 (August 1962) in the Silver Age of Comic Books. He has appeared in American comic books ever since, in his own comic book series, in numerous team-up books, and in other media. He is the most popular and commercially successful character of Marvel Comics. Spider-Man is the alter ego of Peter Parker, a high school student who was orphaned when his parents were killed in a plane crash. Peter lives with his Aunt May and Uncle Ben in New York City. After being bitten by a radioactive spider, Peter gains superhuman strength, speed, and agility, as well as the ability to cling to surfaces and shoot webs from his wrists. He uses his powers to fight crime and protect the innocent. Spider-Man is one of the most popular and iconic superheroes in the world. He has been featured in numerous films, television shows, video games, and other media. He is a member of the superhero team the Avengers and has appeared in many of their adventures. Spider-Man is a beloved character because he is relatable and aspirational. He is a young person who has been given great power, and he chooses to use that power to help others.
